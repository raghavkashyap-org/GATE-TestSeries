Which of the following statements about attribute grammars and their evaluation is FALSE?

Correct answer: B
Solution

(b)
• Synthesized attributes depend only on children nodes and can be computed bottom-up in one pass.
• L-attributed grammars are designed so inherited attributes can be evaluated in a single leftto right top-down pass.
• Some attribute grammars are inherently non-linear or circular and cannot be evaluated in one pass by any parser without extra mechanisms.
• Inherited attributes flow from parents (or left sibling) to children.
