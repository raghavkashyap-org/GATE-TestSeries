Consider the following statements:
S1 : If all edge costs are equal and positive, then Breadth-First Search (BFS) is optimal.
S2 : Breadth-First Search (BFS) has complete and finite time and space complexity for finite search spaces.
Which of the following is correct?

Correct answer: C
Solution

(c)
• BFS expands nodes in order of increasing depth. If all edge costs are equal and positive, path cost increases with depth. Hence, BFS returns the solution with the lowest path cost and is optimal. So, S1 is correct.
• BFS is complete in any finite search space because it systematically explores all nodes at each depth level. Its time and space complexity are finite in such spaces, though both can be exponential in the worst case (Ο(bd)). Thus, it guarantees finding a solution if one exists. So, S2
is correct.

S2 : The Markov blanket of a node consists of all nodes that are reachable within two edges from it.
Which of the following is correct?

Correct answer: A
Solution

(a)
• Markov blanket of a node includes all the nodes that directly affect it or are affected by it — that means its parents, children, and co-parents (other parents of its children). Once you know these, the node has no influence from the rest of the network. So, S1 is correct.
• “All nodes within two edges” is too broad — some of those nodes may have no role in influencing the node. The Markov blanket depends on relationships, not just distance. So, S2 is incorrect.

Correct answer: D
Solution

(d)

Beam Search: Stores only a fixed number (beam width) of best candidates, making it more memory-efficient. So, S2 is incorrect.
