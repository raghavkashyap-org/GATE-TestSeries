Given an array of n integers, where each element is at most d positions away from its correct
position. When applying Merge Sort, which of the following is correct?

Correct answer: A, B, C, D
Solution

(a)
• Merge Sort has a fixed time complexity of Ο(n log n), regardless of whether the array is
nearly sorted. The array being nearly sorted does not affect the overall time complexity but
may improve constant factors (fewer swaps/moves). So, option (a) is correct.
• Merge Sort will still perform efficiently and will not degrade to Ο(n2). So, option (b) is incorrect.
• The time complexity does not change with the value of d. So, option (c) is incorrect.
• Merging will be faster due to fewer moves or swaps in nearly sorted arrays, not slower. So,
option (d) is incorrect.
