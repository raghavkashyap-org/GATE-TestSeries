How does a feedforward neural network differ from other types of neural networks?

Correct answer: D
Solution

(d)
A feedforward neural network is a type of neural network where information flows in one direction, from the input layer through the hidden layers (if present) to the output layer, without any feedback connections or cycles. This means that the output of one layer serves as the input to the next layer, and there are no loops or recurrent connections that would allow information to flow back to previous layers.
