Consider the process of likelihood weighting in approximate Bayesian inference.
(a) Likelihood weighting samples from the prior distribution of variables.
(b) Likelihood weighting assigns weights to samples based on how well they match the observed evidence.
(c) Likelihood weighting always guarantees exact posterior probabilities.
(d) Likelihood weighting is computationally efficient for large Bayesian networks.
Which of the above statements is/are correct?

Correct answer: B, D
Solution

(b, d)
• Likelihood weighting samples from the conditional distribution of variables given the observed evidence, not from the prior distribution. So option (a) is incorrect.
• In likelihood weighting, samples are assigned weights proportional to their likelihood under the observed evidence. This allows for estimation of posterior probabilities. So option (b) is correct.
• Likelihood weighting provides an approximation to the posterior distribution, especially useful when exact inference is computationally infeasible. So option (c) is incorrect.
• Likelihood weighting can be computationally efficient compared to other exact methods, especially for large networks. However, it depends on the complexity of the network and the number of samples required. So option (b) is correct.

(a) Gibbs Sampling requires the joint distribution of variables to be known.
(b) Gibbs Sampling guarantees convergence to the true posterior distribution.
(c) Gibbs Sampling samples all variables simultaneously in each iteration.
(d) Gibbs Sampling can handle high-dimensional distributions effectively.
Which of the above statements is/are correct?

Correct answer: A, D
Solution

(a, d)
• Gibbs Sampling requires knowledge of the joint distribution to sample from the conditional distributions of variables. So, option (a) is correct.
• While Gibbs Sampling is designed to sample from the posterior distribution, convergence to the true posterior depends on factors such as the mixing properties of the sampler and the number of iterations. So, option (b) is incorrect.
• Gibbs Sampling updates variables one at a time based on their conditional distributions. So, option (c) is incorrect.
• Gibbs Sampling is known for its effectiveness in sampling from high-dimensional distributions by sequentially updating variables. So, option (d) is correct.
