Not attempted

Consider the Taylor series  centered at z0 = 2 Which of the following statements are true regarding the radius of convergence?

Correct answer: A, B, D
Solution

(a, b, d)
• If the Taylor series converges at z = 0 the radius of convergence must be at least the distance
from z0 = 2 to z = 0 which is 2. So, option (a) is true.
• If the Taylor series converges at z = 3 the radius of convergence must be at least the distance
from z0 = 2 to z = 3 which is 1. So, option (b) is true.
• It is impossible for the Taylor series to converge at z = 0 and diverge at z = 3 If the series
converges at z = 0 it will also converge at all points within the disk of radius 2, meaning it
would converge at z = 3 as well. So, option (c) is false.
• If the Taylor series converges at z = 0 it will converge at all points within the disk of radius 2
around z0 = 2 meaning it will also converge at points inside this radius. So, option (d) is true.
